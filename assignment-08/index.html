Yeast Buns!

Materials

  • 2-3 Tbsp honey
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1 packet rapid rise yeast (1/4 oz)
  • 1/2 cup room temperature milk
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup canola oil
  • 2 3/4 cups all purpose flour
  • 9x13 inch baking pan - either dark non-stick or glass (Please refer to bottom pictures and desciptions to see the difference when baking in dark non-stick or glass)
  • Large mixing bowl
  • Large coffee mug
  • Dish towel
  • Non-stick spray

Directions

  1. Add yeast packet into coffee mug, add 1/2 warm water, and 2-3 Tbsp honey let sit for about 15 mins to bloom
  2. Add bloomed yeast to large mixing bowl, 1/2 cup milk, 1/2 tsp salt, and 1/4 cup canola oil then mix together
  3. Add 2 3/4 cups all purpose flour to large mixing bowl and mix until a sticky dough forms
  4. Put flour down on counter top and begin to knead dough adding flour as you go until a dough ball forms




  5. Lightly spray large mixing bowl with non-stick spray, put dough ball in it, cover with dish towl, and put it in the oven with the oven light on. Let rise 1 hour
  6. Lightly spray baking dish with non-stick baking pan
  7. After an hour push down dough, take dough out, cut it into 12 equalish parts, form 12 small dough ball shapes




  8. Place dough ball shapes into baking dish, cover with dish towel, and put into oven with oven light on for 1 hour




  9. Take dough out of oven and pre-heat oven 370 degrees
  10. When oven has reached 370 degrees, place baking dish into middle of baking rack, and place timer for 15-17 minutes
  11. Once your desired brown on top of buns is reached, remove from oven
  12. Let cool before touching!
  13. Enjoy how you like! I like to top with butter.




Baking Sheet Type Minutes in Oven Outcome Description
Glass 15 minutes Pale tops, needs more time to bake
Glass 16 minutes Light golden tops, soft inside
Glass 17 minutes Golden tops, soft inside
Dark Non-stick 15 minutes Light golden brown tops, soft inside
Dark Non-stick 16 minutes Golden brown tops, soft inside
Dark Non-stick 17 minutes Dark golden brown tops, soft inside, slightly burnt bottoms
